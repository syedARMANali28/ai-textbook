# Nav2 Chapter Quiz

**1. What is Nav2 primarily designed for in ROS-enabled robots?**
   a) Robot arm manipulation.
   b) Advanced navigation, path planning, and obstacle avoidance.
   c) Human-robot interaction.
   d) Real-time sensor data processing.

**2. Why is adapting Nav2 for humanoid robots particularly challenging?**
   a) Humanoids are too slow to use Nav2 effectively.
   b) Nav2 is incompatible with humanoid sensors.
   c) Humanoids have unique locomotion (bipedal walking), balance control, and interaction with vertical obstacles.
   d) Nav2 only works with wheeled robots.

**3. What role does Isaac ROS play in enhancing Nav2's capabilities for humanoids?**
   a) It provides new algorithms for Nav2.
   b) It generates high-fidelity, real-time environmental data through hardware-accelerated perception for Nav2's use.
   c) It replaces Nav2 entirely for humanoid navigation.
   d) It helps humanoids to communicate with other robots.

**4. Nav2 allows for generation of sophisticated, dynamically adaptable trajectories. What does this mean in the context of humanoid navigation?**
   a) The humanoid will always follow a straight line.
   b) The humanoid can adjust its movement in real-time to avoid obstacles and maintain balance.
   c) The humanoid can only move in pre-programmed patterns.
   d) The humanoid's navigation is entirely random.

---
**Answers:**
1. b)
2. c)
3. b)
4. b)
