# Gazebo Simulation Chapter Quiz

**1. What is Gazebo's primary strength as a robotics simulator?**
   a) Its high-fidelity graphics.
   b) Its robust physics engine and accuracy in modeling physical interactions.
   c) Its ease of use for non-technical users.
   d) Its focus on C# scripting.

**2. Gazebo is widely used within which robotics ecosystem?**
   a) Unity Robotics.
   b) NVIDIA Omniverse.
   c) Robot Operating System (ROS).
   d) Unreal Engine.

**3. What is the "reality gap" in robotics simulation?**
   a) The difference between virtual and augmented reality.
   b) The discrepancy between simulated and real-world robot performance.
   c) The time delay in real-time robot operation.
   d) The gap in knowledge between developers and users.

**4. How does Gazebo's physics engine primarily benefit robotics development?**
   a) By making the robot visually appealing.
   b) By allowing rigorous evaluation of control algorithms and physical interactions.
   c) By providing abstract sensor data.
   d) By completely eliminating the need for real-world testing.

---
**Answers:**
1. b)
2. c)
3. b)
4. b)
